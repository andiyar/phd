# Paper 4 Codebook

Framework analysis codebook — DREAMS Trial qualitative study.
Top-level codes are fixed (deductive). Sub-codes emerge inductively from data.

Last updated: 2026-05-07

## Communication
*Quotes about the patient's communicative capacity — ability to receive or produce communication, including verbal, non-verbal, and gestural acts.*

| Sub-code | Definition | First seen | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Verbal | Patient conscious and communicating freely — answering questions, conversing | DXM19-Nursing DayMinus1 | Approx RASS-PAL 0 to -1 |
| Limited verbal | Patient drowsy or reduced but producing words or short answers | DXM3-Nursing Day1 | Approx RASS-PAL -1 to -2 |
| Responsive to voice | Patient rousable or responsive to verbal stimulation | MDZ9-Nursing Day1 | Approx RASS-PAL -2 to -3 |
| Responsive to touch | Patient responds minimally to physical stimulation but not to voice | DXM19-Nursing Day2 | Approx RASS-PAL -3 to -4 |
| Minimally responsive | Patient intermittently waking or showing minimal spontaneous awareness | DXM24-Nursing Day1 | Approx RASS-PAL -4 |
| Non-responsive | Patient unresponsive to voice and touch | MDZ16-Nursing Day0 | Approx RASS-PAL -5 |
| Communicating needs | Patient verbally or non-verbally communicating a need or request | MDZ23-Medical Day0 | |
| Difficulty communicating | Patient unable to verbalise but retaining some non-verbal capacity (nods, gestures, pointing) | DXM23-PCA Day2 | |
| Confused | Patient alert but cognitively vague, limiting meaningful interaction — dual code with SC (Delirium) when content warrants | DXM10-Nursing DayMinus1 | |
| Change in responsiveness | Documentation of a shift in consciousness level within or between assessments | DXM15-Nursing Day1 | |
| Non-verbal | Documentation of non-verbal behaviours — facial expressions, body movements — without clear communicative intent | MDZ14-PCA Day0 | |

| Agitation | Documentation of agitation episodes, attributed to discomfort or distress | DXM23-PCA Day2 |
| Dizziness | Documentation of dizziness or vertigo | DXM3-PCA Day1 |

## Patient-Centred Care
*Quotes about care that respects the patient as a person — dignity, comfort, preferences, holistic care.*

| Sub-code | Definition | First seen |
|---|---|---|

| Comfort - Self Report | Patient's own verbal report of comfort, wellbeing, or absence of distress | MDZ16-Medical DayMinus1 |
| Emotional wellbeing | Patient experiencing positive emotion or joy, connected to those around them | DXM19-Nursing DayMinus1 |
| Reassurance | Staff providing reassurance to a distressed patient | DXM16-Nursing Day1 |

| Physical comfort | Documentation of hands-on comfort interventions — repositioning, pressure relief, physical adjustments to ease discomfort | DXM3-Nursing Day2 |
