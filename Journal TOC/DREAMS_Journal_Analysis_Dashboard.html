Reading the Matrix – What the Percentages Mean

Each cell shows the publication rate for that methodology at that journal (based on 100% ToC analysis).

Paper 1 row (Protocol): Percentages = proportion of study protocols. Most palliative journals don't publish protocols → JMIR Res Protoc (100% protocols).
Paper 2 row (RCT): Percentages = proportion of RCTs published by each journal. JPSM at 8.1% = highest RCT rate.
Paper 3 row (Secondary Analysis): Percentages = proportion of secondary/sub-analyses. BMJ at 6.2% = highest SA rate.
Paper 4 row (Qualitative): Percentages = proportion of qualitative studies. PallMed at 46.1% = dominant qualitative focus.
Paper 5 row (SLR): Percentages = proportion of systematic reviews. BMJ at 15.9% = highest SR rate.

✓ Green highlight = best fit journal for that methodology. Yellow = viable option. ✗ Red/muted = poor fit or rejected (with actual rate shown).
